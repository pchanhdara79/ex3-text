Notice how in the paragraph above, the bold text in the screen shot draws the eye and helps you find the beginning of the text? This is a common print convention in magazines or newspapers (digital and analog), which helps the sighted viewer find the beginning of a story on a page that has a lot of other information on it (other stories, advertisements, etc). The tag that creates this visual pathway is non-semantic because the bold text doesn't mean anything special; it is just at the beginning, so the visual formatting grabs the eye.
